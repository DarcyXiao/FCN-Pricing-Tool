FCN Pricing — Methodology & Calculation Details

1. Overview

This tool prices Fixed Coupon Notes (FCNs) using Monte Carlo simulation with correlated Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM). Two modes:

  • Fair Value — Given a coupon rate, compute the present value of the FCN.
  • Fair Coupon — Given a target fair value (default 100%), solve for the equilibrium coupon via bisection.

2. Data Preparation

Historical closing prices from Yahoo Finance. From the price series we compute:

$$r_t = \ln\!\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t-1}}\right) \qquad \sigma = \operatorname{std}(r) \times \sqrt{252} \qquad \rho_{ij} = \frac{\operatorname{Cov}(r_i, r_j)}{\sigma_i \cdot \sigma_j}$$
SymbolMeaningComputation
$r_t$Daily log return$\ln(P_t / P_{t-1})$
$\sigma$Annualized volatility$\operatorname{std}(r) \times \sqrt{252}$
$\rho_{ij}$Correlation$\operatorname{Cov}(r_i, r_j) / (\sigma_i^{\text{daily}} \sigma_j^{\text{daily}})$

3. Monte Carlo Simulation — Correlated GBM

Under the risk-neutral measure, each asset follows:

$$\frac{dS_i}{S_i} = r \cdot dt + \sigma_i \cdot dW_i$$

Correlated shocks via Cholesky decomposition:

$$\begin{aligned} \Sigma &= L \cdot L^T &&\text{(Cholesky factorization)} \\[4pt] Z_{\text{corr}} &= L \cdot Z &&(Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)\text{ i.i.d.}) \\[4pt] S_i(t+dt) &= S_i(t) \cdot \exp\!\Bigl((r - \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_i^2)\,dt + \sigma_i\sqrt{dt} \cdot Z_{\text{corr}}[i]\Bigr) \end{aligned}$$

Box-Muller transform for standard normals:

$$Z = \sqrt{-2\ln U_1} \cdot \cos(2\pi U_2), \quad U_1, U_2 \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)$$

Paths simulated at daily granularity ($dt = 1/252$ yr). A product with $M$ months tenor uses $21M + 5$ total steps.

4. Payoff Structure & Discounting

Worst-of basket — all barrier conditions evaluated on the minimum return across assets.

ScenarioConditionDiscounted Payoff
① Knock-Out All assets $\ge$ KO barrier on any day $t \ge$ KO Start $100 \cdot \bigl(1 + c \cdot \frac{T-5}{252}\bigr) \cdot e^{-r \cdot T/252}$
② Safe Maturity No KO; no KI, or KI but worst $\ge$ Strike $100 \cdot \bigl(1 + c \cdot \frac{T_{\text{total}}-5}{252}\bigr) \cdot e^{-r \cdot T_{\text{total}}/252}$
③ Delivery KI breached AND worst $\lt$ Strike at maturity $\bigl(100c\frac{T_{\text{total}}-5}{252} + 100\frac{S_{\text{worst}}}{\text{Strike}}\bigr) e^{-r \cdot T_{\text{total}}/252}$
KI Types:
American (Continuous) — KI observed every day; any breach triggers KI permanently.
European (At Maturity) — KI observed only on the final day.

5. Fair Value

$$\text{Fair Value (\%)} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^{N} PV_k, \qquad \text{SE} \approx \frac{\operatorname{std}(PV)}{\sqrt{N}}$$

6. Fair Coupon Solver — Bisection

The fair coupon $c^*$ satisfies $FV(c^*) = \text{Target}$ (typically 100%). Since $FV(c)$ is monotonic in $c$:

  1. Bounds: $c_{\text{low}} = 0.1\%$, $c_{\text{high}} = 50\%$
  2. Midpoint: $c_{\text{mid}} = (c_{\text{low}} + c_{\text{high}}) / 2$
  3. Run Monte Carlo → get $FV(c_{\text{mid}})$
  4. $FV \gt \text{Target}$ → $c_{\text{high}} = c_{\text{mid}}$; else $c_{\text{low}} = c_{\text{mid}}$
  5. Repeat until $|FV - \text{Target}| \lt 0.05$ or range $\lt 0.01\%$ (max 25 iter)
$$\text{Convergence: } O\!\left(\log_2\frac{\text{range}}{\text{tolerance}}\right) \quad\Rightarrow\quad \lceil \log_2(4990) \rceil \approx 13 \text{ iterations max}$$

7. Assumptions & Limitations

  • Constant volatility — GBM assumes constant $\sigma$; real markets exhibit volatility clustering.
  • Constant correlation — correlations can spike during market stress.
  • No dividends — Yahoo Finance adjusted-close prices are used; explicit dividends not modeled.
  • No jump risk — plain GBM without Merton jump-diffusion; tail risk may be underestimated.
  • Constant risk-free rate — yield curve effects ignored.
  • Daily monitoring — continuous barriers approximated at daily frequency (~21 steps/month).
  • Monte Carlo noise — sampling error $\sim O(1/\sqrt{N})$; 2000 paths → ~2pp SE.
